ABOUT THE PROJECT

The International Union for Conservation of Nature Red List classifies many species of whales and dolphins as threatened in the Mediterranean. Research into cetaceans focuses on their strandings and sightings, there is little knowledge about their movements, whale pod composition and their behaviour. This lack of information makes it difficult to understand the threats to cetaceans and to develop effective conservation strategies.

This objective of the project is to create machine learning models to predict the behaviour of sperm whales in the Mediterranean Sea. Using data from Oceanomare Delphis we aim to learn more about the behaviour of cetaceans, and to use this information to help guide legislation to protect them.

OCEANOMARE DELPHIS

Oceanomare Delphis is a non-profit organisation that gathers data on behavioural ecology and acoustics of cetaceans. Currently their databases hold information on distribution, movements, group sizes and compositions, behaviours and acoustic data. Oceanomare Delphis combines scientific research in the field with priority objectives such as education and conservation and aggregates organizations, groups and individuals linked by a common interest in cetaceans and the marine environment, to influence institutions to take concrete action against the extinction and destruction of natural ecosystems caused by man.

See more of their work at oceanomaredelphis.org
